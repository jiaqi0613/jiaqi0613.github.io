Navigate to inclusivity: Revolutionize digital accessibility management with our simplified solution.

I was selected to work in a team on an exciting and challenging problem entrusted by Warren Walter, ESN involved in digital accessibility. It engaged a group of professionals to work on real business issues using the Design Sprint methodology.

It's the process of going from a problem to a prototype tested by users in just 5 days! We worked in very dynamic and timed workshops.

Detail of the Design Sprint

5 days of Design Sprint to answer a problem given by Warren Walter. We had to create a simple tool for managing digital accessibility to help those in charge of the topic in companies to advance it more easily. We proposed a solution that allows the referent to save time in building their approach, to engage the right actions at the right time, and to follow the progression of these actions all within a tool that progressively goes from theory to practice.

Week 1

  • Phase 1: Definition — Interview with experts, understanding user needs, constructing the problem, long-term objective, obstacles, problem mapping.
  • Phase 2: Ideation in 4 steps — Everyone sketches their most complete solution that would solve the problem.
  • Phase 3: Decision — Series of voting phases, designation of the sketch that carries the idea to be tested, user flow, and storyboard.
  • Phase 4: Prototyping — The creation of the prototype on Figma.
  • Phase 5: User tests — Via video conference.

Week 2

  • Analysis of test feedback and prioritization of corrections
  • Improvement of the prototype
  • Preparation of the presentation before +30 people

Analyze, Understand & Solve the Problem

To answer the problem, our team imagined a solution in 3 steps:

  • A questionnaire to understand where things stand with digital accessibility within the company.
  • Personalized recommendations to integrate into the action plan
  • A flexible template that outlines these key areas, provides detailed tasks and offers tips to help transition the action plan into a collaborative dashboard.

This approach wasn't just about efficiency; it made pinpointing the next right move and tracking our strides forward a breeze.

Our aim was simple: make navigating the complex world of digital accessibility as straightforward as possible. This tool was designed to be a breeze for anyone to use, whether flying solo or collaborating with a crew. It was all about giving each accessibility lead the reins to drive their plan, with a little guidance from our tool to light the way.

We put our creation through its paces with real users over video calls, and that feedback was super valuable. Watching their reactions, and hearing their thoughts, it was a clear sign we were on the right track, keeping our users front and center.

Post-test, we fine-tuned our prototype based on what users told us and put together a detailed wrap-up. This included a run-through of the improved prototype and our next steps.

This whole experience was a crash course in creativity and teamwork from day one. Jumping in with a brand-new team, and facing an unknown problem head-on—it was a rush. Each workshop was a reminder that everyone's got a bit of creative genius in them and that a mix of different viewpoints is the secret sauce to real innovation.
